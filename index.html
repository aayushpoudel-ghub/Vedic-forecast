01 — METHOD

Not a sun-sign horoscope. A real computation.

Most birthday apps map you to one of twelve signs and write the same paragraph for millions of people. This works the way a traditional jyotiṣī works — from the exact positions overhead at your moment of birth.

1

Locate the sky

Your birth date, time, and place fix a precise moment. We resolve the place to coordinates and the historically-correct timezone, then compute sidereal longitudes for all nine grahas and your rising sign.

2

Read the periods

The Moon's nakshatra sets your Vimśottarī daśā — the sequence of planetary periods that timing in Vedic astrology rests on. We find the mahādaśā and antardaśā governing your next five years.

3

Synthesize the forecast

House lords, dignities, the daśā lord's placement, and current transits combine into a specific reading — which life areas are activated, when the openings come, and where to take care.

04 — IN GOOD FAITH

What this can and cannot do.

The astronomy here is exact: the planetary positions and period dates are computed to observatory standards, the same way professional jyotiṣa software does it. That part is real.

The interpretation is a different thing. Astrology is a symbolic, interpretive tradition — not a validated predictive science. A favorable period is best understood as permission, not delivery: it describes conditions under which your own effort tends to pay off, never a guarantee that it will. The chart is a mirror that can sharpen what you already sense — it is not a substitute for your judgment, and never for medical, legal, or financial advice.

Read it the way you'd read a thoughtful letter from someone who knows the tradition: take what rings true, and author the rest yourself.
